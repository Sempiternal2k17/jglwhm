Practicing Forgiveness

Dear teenagers, Today, I want to talk to you about one of the most important virtues that you can practice: forgiveness.

As young people, you are at a stage in life where you may experience hurt and pain from the actions of others. It can be challenging to forgive those who have hurt you, but practicing forgiveness is crucial for your emotional and spiritual well-being.

Forgiveness means letting go of anger, resentment, and bitterness towards those who have hurt you. It's not easy, but it's an essential aspect of our faith as Christians. When we forgive others, we show the love and mercy of Jesus Christ.

In Matthew 6:14-15, Jesus says, "For if you forgive others their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you, but if you do not forgive others their trespasses, neither will your Father forgive your trespasses." Forgiving others is not only the right thing to do, but it also brings us closer to God and helps us grow in our faith.

Forgiveness is not about forgetting what has happened or excusing bad behavior. It's about acknowledging the pain and hurt that we have experienced, and choosing to let go of our negative feelings towards the person who has hurt us.

So, how can we practice forgiveness in our daily lives? Here are a few steps:

Firstly, recognize that forgiveness is a choice. You may not feel like forgiving, but you can choose to do so anyway, knowing that it's the right thing to do.

Secondly, seek God's help in forgiving others. Pray for the grace to let go of anger and bitterness, and ask for the strength to forgive as Christ forgave us.

Lastly, remember that forgiveness is not always a one-time event. It may take time and effort to forgive someone fully, but through prayer, faith, and humility, it can be achieved.

As you go through life, you will experience hurt and pain from others, but if you choose to forgive, you can find healing and freedom from the pain. So, my dear teenagers, I encourage you to practice forgiveness in your daily lives. Choose to let go of anger and bitterness, seek God's help in forgiving others, and humbly extend grace to those who have hurt you. May God bless you and give you the strength to forgive as He has forgiven us. Amen.
