What is Salvation?

Speaker: Rev. Alexander V. Isaguirre

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."

John 3:16

Good day, brothers and sisters in Christ. Today, I want to talk to you about the most precious gift that God has given us - the gift of salvation.

Salvation is the central message of the gospel, and it is the reason why Jesus came to earth, died on the cross, and rose from the dead. Salvation is the way that God rescues us from sin and death and gives us eternal life.

So, what is salvation? Simply put, salvation is the act of being saved or rescued from something. In the context of Christianity, salvation is being saved from the consequences of our sin and rebellion against God. Because of our sin, we are separated from God, and we face eternal punishment in hell. But God, in His mercy and grace, has provided a way for us to be saved - through faith in Jesus Christ.

In Romans 6:23, the Bible says, "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." This verse shows us that salvation is a gift - it is something that we do not deserve or earn, but that God freely gives to us because of His love for us.

How do we receive this gift of salvation? The Bible tells us that we must believe in Jesus Christ as our Lord and Savior, repent of our sins, and confess our faith in Him. When we do this, we are forgiven of our sins and reconciled to God. We become new creations, born again into a new life in Christ.

But salvation is not just a one-time event - it is a lifelong journey of growing in our relationship with God and living out His will for our lives. As we follow Jesus, we are transformed by the power of the Holy Spirit, and we become more like Christ in our thoughts, words, and actions.

So, I encourage you today, if you have not yet received the gift of salvation, to come to Jesus in faith and surrender your life to Him. And if you have already received salvation, to continue to walk in faith and obedience, trusting in God's love and grace to carry you through the ups and downs of life.
