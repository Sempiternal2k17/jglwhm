Pursuing Intimacy in Marriage

Nurturing Emotional, Physical, and Spiritual Intimacy with Your Spouse

Today, I want to speak to you about pursuing intimacy in marriage. As Christians, we understand that marriage is not just a legal contract or a social arrangement, but a sacred covenant designed by God. And one of the key elements of that covenant is intimacy.

However, intimacy is not just about physical closeness, but also emotional and spiritual closeness. In order to truly experience intimacy in marriage, we must nurture all three types of intimacy: emotional, physical, and spiritual.

First, let us talk about emotional intimacy. Emotional intimacy is the closeness and connection we feel with our spouse on an emotional level. This means that we should be willing to share our thoughts, feelings, and experiences with our spouse, and to listen and support them in return.

This can be challenging, especially if we have been hurt or let down in the past, but it is essential for building trust and deepening our connection with our spouse. When we pursue emotional intimacy with our spouse, we create a safe and supportive environment that allows us to grow together and overcome any challenges that come our way.

Second, let us talk about physical intimacy. Physical intimacy is the closeness and connection we feel with our spouse on a physical level. This means that we should be willing to express our love and affection through physical touch and intimacy, and to prioritize our spouse's needs and desires.

However, physical intimacy is not just about pleasure or fulfillment, but also about the deeper emotional and spiritual connection that is created through physical intimacy. When we approach physical intimacy with a spirit of love and sacrifice, we create a deeper bond with our spouse that goes beyond just physical attraction.

Finally, let us talk about spiritual intimacy. Spiritual intimacy is the closeness and connection we feel with our spouse on a spiritual level. This means that we should be willing to pray together, to worship together, and to share our faith with one another.

When we pursue spiritual intimacy with our spouse, we create a deeper understanding of each other's values and beliefs, and we strengthen our relationship with God as well. This type of intimacy is essential for building a strong foundation for our marriage and for facing any challenges that come our way.

In conclusion, let us remember that intimacy is not just about physical closeness, but also emotional and spiritual closeness. When we pursue intimacy in all three areas, we create a strong and lasting connection with our spouse that can withstand any trial or difficulty. So let us commit ourselves to nurturing emotional, physical, and spiritual intimacy with our spouse, and to being a testimony of God's love and grace to the world around us.

May God bless you and your marriages abundantly. Amen.
