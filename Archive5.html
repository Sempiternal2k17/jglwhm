Guarding Our Heart

Ps. Emy Isaguirre

"Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it."

Proverbs 4:23

Dear brothers and sisters in Christ, today I want to talk to you about the importance of guarding our heart. Our heart is the seat of our emotions, desires, and motives. It is the source of our thoughts, words, and actions. And so, it is crucial that we guard our heart, for everything we do flows from it.

The Bible tells us that our heart is deceitful and wicked (Jeremiah 17:9). It is prone to wander and to follow after the desires of the flesh. That is why we need to guard our heart, to protect it from the temptations and deceptions of the world.

So, how do we guard our heart? First and foremost, we need to fill our heart with the truth of God's Word. The Bible tells us that it is the "sword of the Spirit" (Ephesians 6:17), and it is able to pierce through the lies and deceptions of the enemy. When we meditate on God's Word and hide it in our heart, we are equipped to discern right from wrong and to resist temptation.

Secondly, we need to be mindful of what we allow into our heart. The things we watch, listen to, and read can have a profound impact on our heart and mind. We need to be careful not to fill our heart with things that are contrary to God's Word, such as violent or sexually explicit media, gossip, and negativity. Instead, we should seek to fill our heart with things that are pure, true, and honorable (Philippians 4:8).

Thirdly, we need to surround ourselves with godly influences. The people we spend time with can greatly influence our heart and our behavior. We need to choose friends and mentors who will encourage us in our walk with Christ and hold us accountable to God's standards.

In conclusion, let us guard our heart above all else. Let us fill it with God's Word, be mindful of what we allow in, and surround ourselves with godly influences. When we do so, we are able to resist temptation and live a life that honors God.
